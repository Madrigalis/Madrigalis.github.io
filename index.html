About

During the 16th-17th century, lyrics by the most influential Italian poets used to be set to music and played at courts for people’s entertainment. As during Renaissance words’ meaning was amplified by the power of these musical compositions, Giving voice to Madrigals is an attempt to draw attention to this musical genre through a visual data journey.

TIP: Navigate with the mouse over the paintings and let them recount their music. They’ve been silent for soooo long!

The Project

This project was undertaken for the Electronic Publishing and Digital Storytelling (a.y. 2021-2022) final examination, as part of the Digital Humanities and Digital Knowledge Master's Degree at the University of Bologna.

madrigal (noun)
ˈma-dri-gəl
A genre of musical composition, extremely popular in the 16th-17th century, for voice ensemble or solo voice and instruments. Its distinguishing feature is word-painting (madrigalism): the expression, through the means of music, of the lyrics’ affects and meaning.
